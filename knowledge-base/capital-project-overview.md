# Capital Project Overview - IBM Maximo Real Estate & Facilities

## What is a Capital Project?

A **Capital Project** in MREF/TRIRIGA represents a significant investment in real estate or facilities infrastructure. These are typically large-scale initiatives that require:
- Substantial budget allocation
- Multi-phase execution
- Cross-functional coordination
- Regulatory compliance
- Long-term asset impact

## Capital Project Lifecycle

### 1. **Planning Phase**
- **Capital Program Creation**: Strategic planning at portfolio level
- **Project Identification**: Define scope, objectives, and business case
- **Budget Estimation**: Initial cost analysis and funding allocation
- **Feasibility Study**: Technical and financial viability assessment

### 2. **Approval Phase**
- **Proposal Submission**: Formal project proposal with justification
- **Review Process**: Multi-level approval workflow
- **Budget Authorization**: Financial commitment and allocation
- **Stakeholder Sign-off**: Executive and departmental approvals

### 3. **Execution Phase**
- **Project Initiation**: Kick-off and team formation
- **Design & Engineering**: Technical specifications and drawings
- **Procurement**: RFP/RFQ/Bid processes for contractors
- **Construction/Implementation**: Physical work execution
- **Quality Control**: Inspections and compliance verification

### 4. **Monitoring Phase**
- **Progress Tracking**: Milestone completion and schedule adherence
- **Budget Management**: Cost tracking and variance analysis
- **Risk Management**: Issue identification and mitigation
- **Change Management**: Scope changes and impact assessment

### 5. **Closeout Phase**
- **Final Inspections**: Completion verification
- **Documentation**: As-built drawings and warranties
- **Financial Reconciliation**: Final cost accounting
- **Asset Transfer**: Handover to operations/maintenance
- **Lessons Learned**: Post-project review

## Key Components

### Capital Programs
- **Definition**: Portfolio-level grouping of related capital projects
- **Purpose**: Strategic alignment and resource optimization
- **Hierarchy**: Program → Projects → Tasks
- **Attributes**: Multi-year planning, budget pools, strategic objectives

### Capital Projects
- **Definition**: Individual initiatives within a capital program
- **Scope**: Specific deliverables with defined start/end dates
- **Budget**: Allocated funding with cost tracking
- **Status**: Active, On Hold, Completed, Cancelled

### Project Tasks
- **Definition**: Granular work items within a project
- **Dependencies**: Predecessor/successor relationships
- **Resources**: Labor, materials, equipment assignments
- **Tracking**: Progress percentage, actual vs. planned

## Project Types

### 4. **Compliance Projects**

## Financial Management

### Budget Categories
- **Original Budget**: Initial approved funding
- **Revised Budget**: Adjusted after change orders
- **Committed Costs**: Purchase orders and contracts
- **Actual Costs**: Invoiced and paid amounts
- **Forecast to Complete**: Projected final cost

### Cost Tracking
- **Cost Codes**: Categorization by work type
- **Cost Centers**: Organizational responsibility
- **Funding Sources**: Capital reserves, bonds, grants
- **Variance Analysis**: Budget vs. actual reporting

## Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

### Schedule Performance
- **On-Time Completion Rate**: % of projects completed by target date
- **Schedule Variance**: Planned vs. actual timeline
- **Milestone Achievement**: Critical path progress

### Budget Performance
- **Cost Variance**: Budget vs. actual spending
- **Budget Utilization**: % of allocated funds spent
- **Cost Performance Index (CPI)**: Earned value metric

### Quality Metrics
- **Defect Rate**: Issues identified during inspections
- **Rework Percentage**: Corrective work required
- **Compliance Score**: Regulatory adherence

### Risk Metrics
- **Risk Exposure**: Potential impact of identified risks
- **Issue Resolution Time**: Average time to close issues
- **Change Order Rate**: Frequency of scope changes

## Best Practices

### Planning
- Comprehensive scope definition
- Realistic schedule development
- Contingency planning (10-15% budget reserve)
- Early stakeholder engagement

### Execution
- Regular status reporting (weekly/bi-weekly)
- Proactive risk management
- Change control discipline
- Quality assurance checkpoints

### Communication
- Stakeholder communication plan
- Regular project meetings
- Transparent reporting
- Issue escalation protocols

### Documentation
- Centralized document repository
- Version control
- Audit trail maintenance
- Knowledge capture

## Common Challenges

### Budget Overruns
- **Causes**: Scope creep, unforeseen conditions, market fluctuations
- **Mitigation**: Rigorous change control, contingency reserves, early warning systems

### Schedule Delays
- **Causes**: Weather, permitting, resource availability, dependencies
- **Mitigation**: Buffer time, critical path management, parallel activities

### Scope Changes
- **Causes**: Evolving requirements, design errors, stakeholder requests
- **Mitigation**: Formal change process, impact analysis, approval gates

### Resource Constraints
- **Causes**: Competing priorities, skill shortages, budget limitations
- **Mitigation**: Resource planning, prioritization, external partnerships
