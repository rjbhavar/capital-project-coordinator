# MREF/TRIRIGA Glossary

## A

**Actual Cost**
- Total costs incurred to date on a project or task
- Includes labor, materials, equipment, and overhead
- Tracked against budget for variance analysis

**Approval Workflow**
- Multi-step process for authorizing projects, changes, or expenditures
- Typically involves multiple stakeholders and approval levels
- Enforces governance and compliance requirements

**Asset**
- Physical or intangible property owned by the organization
- Examples: Buildings, equipment, land, intellectual property
- Tracked for lifecycle management and financial reporting

---

## B

**Budget**
- Financial allocation for a project or program
- Types: Original, Revised, Committed, Actual
- Subject to approval and change control processes

**Building**
- Physical structure or facility
- Contains spaces, floors, and systems
- Associated with capital projects and maintenance activities

**Business Unit**
- Organizational division or department
- Has its own budget, resources, and objectives
- Hierarchical structure within enterprise

---

## C

**Capital Program**
- Strategic portfolio of related capital projects
- Multi-year planning horizon
- Aligned with organizational objectives

**Capital Project**
- Significant investment in real estate or facilities
- Typically exceeds capitalization threshold (e.g., $50K+)
- Results in asset creation or improvement

**Change Order**
- Formal modification to project scope, schedule, or budget
- Requires approval based on impact magnitude
- Tracked for audit and variance analysis

**Classification**
- Predefined list of values for a field (dropdown)
- Examples: Status, Priority, Risk Level, Project Type
- Ensures data consistency and enables reporting

**Commitment**
- Financial obligation through purchase orders or contracts
- Reserved against budget but not yet paid
- Tracked separately from actual costs

**Contingency**
- Budget reserve for unforeseen circumstances
- Typically 10-15% of project budget
- Released through formal change control

**Cost Code**
- Categorization system for expenses
- Examples: Labor, Materials, Equipment, Overhead
- Enables detailed financial analysis

**CRUD**
- Create, Read, Update, Delete operations
- Standard database operations
- Supported by OSLC API

---

## D

**Dashboard**
- Visual display of key metrics and KPIs
- Real-time or near-real-time data
- Customizable by role and preferences

**Dependency**
- Relationship between tasks or activities
- Types: Finish-to-Start, Start-to-Start, Finish-to-Finish
- Critical for schedule management

**Document Management**
- System for storing and organizing project documents
- Version control and access permissions
- Integration with capital projects

---

## E

**Earned Value Management (EVM)**
- Project performance measurement methodology
- Compares planned vs. actual progress and costs
- Key metrics: CPI (Cost Performance Index), SPI (Schedule Performance Index)

**Entity**
- Business object or data record
- Examples: Project, Building, Organization, Person
- Has attributes (fields) and relationships

**Execution Phase**
- Active implementation of project work
- Follows planning and approval phases
- Includes design, procurement, construction

---

## F

**Facilities Management**
- Ongoing operation and maintenance of buildings
- Includes space management, maintenance, utilities
- Integrated with capital project planning

**Feasibility Study**
- Analysis of project viability
- Considers technical, financial, and operational factors
- Informs go/no-go decision

**Funding Source**
- Origin of project budget
- Examples: Capital reserves, bonds, grants, operating budget
- May have restrictions or requirements

---

## G

**Gantt Chart**
- Visual timeline of project schedule
- Shows tasks, durations, dependencies, and milestones
- Common project management tool

**GL Account**
- General Ledger account code
- Links project costs to financial system
- Required for financial reporting

---

## H

**HVAC**
- Heating, Ventilation, and Air Conditioning
- Common capital project type
- Critical building system

---

## I

**Integration**
- Connection between MREF and other systems
- Examples: Financial systems, HR, procurement
- Typically via APIs or file transfers

**Invoice**
- Bill from vendor or contractor
- Triggers payment and cost recording
- Matched to purchase orders

---

## J

**JSESSIONID**
- Session identifier cookie
- Used for authentication in MREF/TRIRIGA
- Must be included in API requests

---

## K

**KPI (Key Performance Indicator)**
- Measurable value demonstrating effectiveness
- Examples: On-time completion rate, budget variance
- Used for performance monitoring and reporting

---

## L

**Lease**
- Rental agreement for property or space
- Tracked in MREF for portfolio management
- May trigger capital improvement projects

**Lifecycle**
- Stages from inception to disposal
- Examples: Project lifecycle, asset lifecycle
- Each stage has specific activities and deliverables

---

## M

**Milestone**
- Significant project event or deliverable
- Zero-duration task in schedule
- Used for progress tracking and reporting

**MREF**
- Maximo Real Estate and Facilities
- IBM's integrated workplace management system
- Formerly known as TRIRIGA

---

## N

**Namespace**
- Prefix for OSLC field names
- Examples: `spi:`, `tri:`, `oslc:`
- Prevents naming conflicts

---

## O

**OSLC**
- Open Services for Lifecycle Collaboration
- REST API standard used by MREF
- Enables integration and data exchange

**Original Budget**
- Initial approved project funding
- Baseline for variance analysis
- Changed only through formal revision process

---

## P

**Phase**
- Distinct stage in project lifecycle
- Examples: Planning, Design, Construction, Closeout
- May have phase gates or approvals

**Portfolio**
- Collection of programs and projects
- Managed for strategic alignment and resource optimization
- Provides enterprise-level visibility

**Predecessor**
- Task that must complete before another can start
- Defines task dependencies
- Critical for schedule logic

**Priority**
- Ranking of importance or urgency
- Used for resource allocation decisions
- Typically numeric (1=highest) or categorical (High/Medium/Low)

**Procurement**
- Process of acquiring goods and services
- Includes RFP, bidding, contracting
- Integrated with project execution

**Purchase Order (PO)**
- Formal commitment to purchase
- Creates budget commitment
- Basis for invoice matching

---

## Q

**Query**
- Request for data from system
- Uses OSLC query syntax
- Can include filters, sorting, field selection

---

## R

**Real Estate**
- Land and buildings owned or leased
- Core focus of MREF system
- Includes portfolio management and transactions

**Resource**
- Person, equipment, or material needed for work
- Allocated to tasks and projects
- Tracked for capacity planning

**Revised Budget**
- Updated budget after approved changes
- Reflects change orders and adjustments
- Current baseline for variance analysis

**RFP (Request for Proposal)**
- Solicitation for vendor bids
- Part of procurement process
- Includes scope, requirements, evaluation criteria

**Risk**
- Potential event that could impact project
- Assessed for probability and impact
- Managed through mitigation strategies

---

## S

**Scope**
- Defined boundaries of project work
- Includes deliverables and exclusions
- Managed through change control

**Service Provider**
- OSLC API endpoint collection
- Groups related resources
- Example: Capital Project Service Provider

**Space**
- Individual room or area within building
- Has area, capacity, and use type
- Assigned to organizations or people

**Stakeholder**
- Individual or group with project interest
- Examples: Sponsor, PM, users, vendors
- Requires communication and engagement

**Status**
- Current state of project, task, or entity
- Examples: Planning, Active, Completed, On Hold
- Drives workflow and reporting

**Successor**
- Task that follows another in sequence
- Opposite of predecessor
- Part of dependency relationship

---

## T

**Task**
- Discrete work item within project
- Has duration, resources, and dependencies
- Rolls up to project level

**TRIRIGA**
- Original name for IBM's IWMS platform
- Now part of Maximo suite (MREF)
- Still used in technical documentation

---

## U

**Use Type**
- Classification of space usage
- Examples: Office, Conference Room, Storage
- Affects space planning and allocation

---

## V

**Variance**
- Difference between planned and actual
- Types: Schedule variance, cost variance
- Key metric for project control

**Vendor**
- External supplier of goods or services
- Managed through contracts and POs
- Performance tracked for future selection

---

## W

**Work Order**
- Request for maintenance or repair work
- Separate from capital projects
- May trigger capital project if major work needed

**Workflow**
- Automated business process
- Examples: Approval routing, status transitions
- Enforces business rules

---

## Status Values Reference

### Project Status
- **Planning** - Initial planning and scoping
- **Approved** - Authorized to proceed
- **Active** / **In Progress** - Work underway
- **On Hold** - Temporarily suspended
- **Completed** - Successfully finished
- **Cancelled** - Terminated before completion
- **Closed** - Administratively closed

### Task Status
- **Not Started** - Scheduled but not begun
- **In Progress** - Currently being worked
- **Completed** - Finished
- **Blocked** - Cannot proceed due to dependency
- **Cancelled** - No longer required

### Document Status
- **Draft** - Work in progress
- **Under Review** - Submitted for approval
- **Approved** - Authorized for use
- **Obsolete** - No longer current
- **Archived** - Retained for historical purposes
